Why We Built This

More Than a Website

Indian street food is not just sustenance â€” it is the autobiography of a civilisation. Every city has its own flavour identity. Every vendor carries generations of culinary knowledge passed down through families. A samosa in Lucknow tastes nothing like one in Bangalore. Pani puri water in Mumbai is not the same as Delhi's golgappa. These distinctions matter deeply.

This project began as a university Git workflow assignment â€” but it became something more meaningful. We wanted to create a resource that genuinely celebrates the diversity and depth of India's street food culture, reaching into states and regions that mainstream food media consistently overlooks: the smoked pork of Nagaland, the bamboo-cooked dishes of Manipur, the coconut-sweet bread of Mangalore.

Every dish on this site was researched, written, and designed by a team member working independently on their own Git branch â€” a small but real reflection of how collaborative software development works in the real world.
